Updating Wireless Drivers

This is important especially for computers that have Intel wireless cards. Legacy Intel drivers do not function well with the wireless infrastructure installed at Williams. Each computer has a Network Interface Card (NIC) that allows your computer to connect to the network or the Internet. Most laptops have one card for wired access and another card for wireless access. A driver program controls how the card operates. As a manufacturer makes improvements to their NIC's, they send out a new driver for you to load. The Williams wireless network uses modern features, and you may need to update your driver to have the best experience. Sometimes a brand new computer will have a driver a few years old.

If you have a computer running the Windows operating system, please check to see if you have Intel Wireless drivers. This can be found by going to:

  • Control Panel > System > Hardware > Device Manager
  • You can search for Device Manager in the control panel of Windows Vista and Windows 7
  • If you have an Intel wireless card, please update the drivers by going to: http://www.intel.com/support/wireless/wlan/sb/cs-010623.htm

Disable Power Saving Mode for the Wireless Card

Some operating system/computer combinations are capable of throttling the power delivered to a wireless card when it is either not heavily used or when the computer is in sleep mode. This can cause some connection problems or result is slower connectivity. If you're having trouble with the wireless network, please try to disable this feature.

  1. Right-click on My Computer (Vista/Win7) and choose Properties
  2. On the System Properties window, click the Hardware tab, then the Device Manager link (Vista/Win7).
  3. Find your wireless network adapter in the list under Network Adapters.
  4. Right-click on the wireless adapter and choose Properties.
  5. Select the Advanced tab and choose Transmit Power in the Property box.
  6. Choose 5. highest from the drop-down box for maximum performance
  7. Select the Power Management tab and make sure there is NO check in the box for Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power, then click OK.
  8. Turn off your wireless transmitter and turn it back on for the change to take effect. On some machines, this can be done by a physical switch on the laptop. On others, it can be done by a combination of keyboard keys. Restarting your computer is generally not necessary, but if you are unsure of how to reset the wireless adapter, you can reboot the computer to make the change take effect.

Radio Hardware and Software Switches

Each wireless card and computer manufacturer have a different way of disabling the radio or wireless card antenna. If you are receiving very little or no signal strength from the Williams wireless networks, please be sure that your radio is on. For PCs, first look for a hardware switch on your device. Some devices implement a hardware switch using the "Fn" key. Hold down "Fn" and press the key with a radio tower. You should get a message telling you if you have just turned WiFi on or off. Refer your laptop manual for more information. For Macs, on the top bar of your screen there is a signal icon. If you click it, it should say Wifi is on.

Disable IPv6

Williams OIT is noticing that some computers are erroneously advertising IPv6 services to others on the wireless networks. This can be caused by a computer having Internet Connection Sharing enabled. When this is the case, users affected by these advertisements are unable to get to some web sites and network services.

  1. Open the Network and Sharing Center
    2. You can do this by first clicking the Windows icon in the lower left hand corner, then typing "Network and Sharing Center" into the search box at the bottom and selecting it from the list.
  2. Click on Change Adapter Settings on the left hand side
  3. Right click on your wireless adapter and click Properties
  4. Uncheck Internet Protocol Version 6 (TCP/IPv6)

Tip: Microsoft has a "Fix-it" tool that will do this for you. Please see: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/9298

Waking Device from "Sleep" Mode

Issue: If you use wireless in one location, put your laptop/device into "Sleep" mode, and move to another location, you may experience difficulty connecting to wireless in the new location. The reason for this is that the device "remembers" the previous location and tries to restore it. Since that location is now out of range, the connection fails. Certain Macbooks appear particularly prone to this behavior.

Solution: The fastest way to restore connectivity is to connect to a different Williams wireless network other than the one you were previously on. This will force your computer to connect from the new location. You should now be able to reconnect to the Williams network.

Wireless Speed Tests

When troubleshooting slow Internet connections, it is useful to gather speed test information, as this helps to tell where the Internet speed bottleneck issues are. NOTE: Both speed tests below require flash to be installed on the device. iOS devices do not support flash.

Local Speed Test

  1. Go to speedtest.williams.edu
  2. Click on Begin Test
  3. Wait for the test to complete
  4. Write down or tell the Williams Helpdesk employee the upload and download speeds
  5. Click the refresh on your browser, run the local speed test twice more, and note the upload and download speeds

Off-Campus Speed Test

  1. Go to http://www.speedtest.net/
  2. Click on Begin Test
  3. Wait for the test to complete
  4. Write down or tell the Williams Helpdesk employee the upload and download speeds, and the location (located in the lower right)
  5. Click the Test Again button, run the speed test twice more, and note the upload and download speeds

Reporting A Problem to OIT

Williams IT is committed to resolving problems and concerns that are reported on the wireless network. Since there can be many different types of problems on the wireless network, Williams IT needs some additional information to help resolve them and determine if there are any trends. You can go to the homepage and fill out the questioner; here are some general questions we need to know.

Overview of troubleshooting steps and Information requested:

  • Provide your name and contact information
  • Update wireless drivers
  • Provide the building name and room number where you are located
  • Provide your MAC address, SSID (network name) - all included in Windows Vista/7 and Mac OS X UofL script (below)
  • Provide the date and time the problem occurred

Description of the Problem (Examples)

  • The connection is slow, intermittent or never connects?
  • "I can get connected in some places but not in others"
  • "The signal strength indicator is showing low"
  • Any other details you can think of
